# JRW Creative Group LLC — Website Specification
### Landing Page Brief for Development, SEO & GEO
**Prepared for:** Paul & Stephen (web/SEO build team)
**Prepared by:** Jaime (Founder, JRW Creative Group LLC)
**Document purpose:** Define exact content, structure, services, and local-search targeting for the JRW Creative Group landing page. This page covers two intertwined arms of the business — **JRW Tech Consulting** and **The Swap** — which are intentionally presented as one brand.

---

## 1. Brand Framing

JRW Creative Group LLC operates under the DBA **The Swap**. The site presents a single unified brand with two service arms that are deliberately inseparable:

1. **The Consulting Arm** — in-home tech design, A/V, server/LLM installation, and retro hardware work (premium, relationship-driven, quote-based).
2. **The Swap** — community makerspace activity: monthly events, 3D printing service, pop-up workshops, and upgraded retro hardware for sale.

**Tone:** Creative, capable, community-first, empowering. The throughline of the entire brand is **empowerment** — giving people the tools, skills, and gear to do things themselves, or having them done right.

**Primary CTA across the whole site:** *Request a free consultation / Get a quote.*

## 2. Consulting Arm — In-Home Tech Design & Beyond

### 2.1 Engagement model (this is the spine of the consulting page)

Every consulting engagement follows one path:

1. **Free 1-hour upfront consultation.** No cost, no obligation. This is the universal entry point and the primary conversion goal for the consulting side of the site.
2. After the consult, the client chooses one of two delivery models:
   - **Flat-rate remote design** — a fixed-scope design package delivered remotely (plans, specs, sourcing lists, configuration guidance).
   - **Local in-home installation** — hourly labor **plus** pass-through cost of equipment and any additional labor.

### 2.2 Pricing presentation: REQUEST FOR QUOTE

**Do not publish dollar figures anywhere on the consulting pages.** Projects vary too widely for a single rate to be meaningful. Every project is quoted individually after the free consultation.

- Site language should make the free consult the obvious, frictionless first step.
- "Equipment and additional labor billed at cost" should be stated plainly so clients understand the install model up front.
- SEO benefit of this approach: the free consult is the conversion event; the page optimizes for lead capture, not price shopping.

### 2.3 Services to list

Present these as example capabilities, not a fixed menu (every build is bespoke):

- **Advanced home theaters** — projector and screen design, calibration, acoustics, room layout.
- **Studios** — audio/creative workspace design and build.
- **A/V routing** — whole-system signal routing and integration.
- **In-home server installation:**
  - **Local LLM setups** (with or without speaker/microphone/voice integration)
  - **NAS servers** (storage, backup, ZFS arrays)
  - **Local media servers**
- **Retro setups** — aesthetic design, sourcing of rare hardware, and modding (both hardware and software) to build the "ultimate" setup for any console.

Framing line for the retro/server work: emphasize *the ultimate, fully-realized setup* — bespoke, high-craft, done right.

### 2.4 Service area (CRITICAL for SEO/GEO)

**Coverage corridor: Philadelphia, PA → Princeton, NJ.**

- Base of operations: Fieldsboro / Bordentown area, Burlington County, NJ.
- This corridor defines the local-search / GEO targeting. The site should rank for in-home tech, home theater, A/V, and server-install queries across this corridor — Burlington County and Mercer County NJ towns plus the Philadelphia metro edge.
- **Competitive note for the build team:** avoid positioning/keywords that directly encroach on FUBAR's clientele. They occupy their own niche; we differentiate on the creative/bespoke/retro and local-LLM angle rather than competing head-to-head.

## 3. The Swap

### 3.1 Events — Year One

- **Cadence:** Monthly events in year one.
- **Conventions:** Booths at conventions when Jaime is able to attend. **The site must be easy to update** with convention dates/booths as they're confirmed — build this as an editable "Upcoming Events / Find Us" module, not hard-coded.
- **Entry model:** Donation-based at first, transitioning to paid entry over time. Site copy should reflect "donation-based" now and be easy to switch to ticketed later.
- **Locations:** None lined up yet. Write as "locations announced per event — join our list / follow us to know where we'll be next."

### 3.2 3D Printing & Equipment — Print Service

- **List this as an active service.** Equipment in hand: Bambu Lab P1P now, with the **P1S (+ AMS 2 Pro) on invoice/incoming.**
- **Model:** Print-for-hire service (we run the job), NOT machine rental.
- **Pricing:** Quote-based / per-project. **Do not publish a flat rate.** Pricing depends on the **actual model, volume, AND complexity** — explicitly note that two prints of the same volume can differ in price when one is more complex.
- Site language: "Pricing based on model, size, and complexity — send us your file or idea for a quote."
- Leave room in the layout to add a published rate card later once Jaime has run the cost math.

### 3.3 Pop-Up Makerspace Workshops

Workshops are the heart of The Swap's community mission. **Theme: empowerment.** Topics span everything creative and practical:

- 3D printing
- Modding (hardware & software)
- Art
- Makeup
- Crochet / knitting
- Soldering
- **"How To Life" series** — a flagship, signature workshop line with a **neurodivergent focus**: learning to make or fix everyday life things ("here's how to fix a broken X," basic car maintenance if/when the right instructor is found, etc.). The mission of this series is to **empower people** with practical life skills in an accessible, ND-friendly way.

**Build notes:**
- Feature "How To Life" as a named, recurring series — it's a differentiator and strong content/SEO anchor.
- Workshops run at events / partner venues / pop-ups in year one (no fixed home yet).
- Ticketing/per-seat pricing TBD — build an editable workshop listing module; no fixed prices in this spec.

### 3.4 Upgraded Retro Hardware (for sale)

- Jaime restores and mods consoles on her own time; these are sold through The Swap.
- **Listing label:** "Upgraded Retro Hardware" (or similar), presented as **"Contact for current offerings"** — relationship-based, no public price list.
- **Compliance framing (important for the build team):** All builds are piracy-free. Listings must center restoration, customization, and quality — never piracy, preloaded copyrighted content, or circumvention as a selling point. Jaime sources and rips her own discs and uses her own ROMs; the site reflects clean, legitimate custom hardware only.
